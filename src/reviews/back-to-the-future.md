**One-line take:** A near-perfect movie that works as comedy, thriller, and love letter to American optimism — all at 88 miles per hour.

**Why it works:**
- The plot mechanics are airtight: every scene plants something that pays off later
- Michael J. Fox and Christopher Lloyd have chemistry that feels genuinely warm and earned
- Alan Silvestri's score is essentially a third character — try watching the skateboard chase without it
- It trusts the audience to keep up without hand-holding or clunky exposition

**Retro rating:** ★★★★★ / 5 neon stars

{% spoiler %}
The ending reveal that Doc has been wearing a bulletproof vest rewires the entire emotional climax — it's technically a cheat, but it works completely because the friendship between Doc and Marty has been made to feel real. You'd forgive Doc anything by that point.
{% endspoiler %}

**One-line take:** Beneath the romance is a surprisingly pointed film about class, bodily autonomy, and what it means to finally be seen — and the dancing is transcendent.

**Why it works:**
- Patrick Swayze plays Johnny Castle with real vulnerability, not just cool-guy posturing
- The period soundtrack is genuinely great, not just nostalgic filler — "Do You Love Me" goes off every time
- Baby's arc is believable and hard-won; she earns her transformation rather than having it handed to her
- The "I've had the time of my life" finale is so well-staged that it remains one of the great feel-good movie endings

**Retro rating:** ★★★★☆ / 5 neon stars

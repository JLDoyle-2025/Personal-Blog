**One-line take:** Dense, difficult, and absolutely worth it — Gibson invented an entire aesthetic that the internet spent decades trying to live up to.

**Why it works:**
- The prose is maximally evocative; every paragraph is doing double duty as world-building and atmosphere
- The world is woven in through texture, not exposition — Gibson trusts you to piece it together
- Case and Molly remain two of the most iconic protagonists in all of genre fiction
- The ending earns its ambiguity — the mystery of what Wintermute actually becomes haunts you afterward

**Retro rating:** ★★★★☆ / 5 neon stars

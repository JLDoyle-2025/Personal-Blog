**One-line take:** The most purely effective horror film of the 1980s — its practical effects remain stomach-dropping and its paranoia never releases its grip.

**Why it works:**
- The ensemble cast means you genuinely do not know who to trust, at any point
- Rob Bottin's creature effects are grotesque and inventive in equal measure — nothing looks like CGI because nothing is
- Ennio Morricone's minimalist score uses silence and a single pulsing bass note as weapons
- The ambiguous ending is one of cinema's great final shots — it earns every second of it

**Retro rating:** ★★★★★ / 5 neon stars

{% spoiler %}
MacReady's blood-test scene is a masterclass in tension construction. The audience knows how the trick works from the moment he explains it, which makes watching each character's petri dish even more unbearable. When Palmer's blood jumps, the entire room erupts — and then Carpenter cuts to a wide shot of the chaos and just lets it breathe.
